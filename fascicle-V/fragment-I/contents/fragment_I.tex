\footnote[1]{An \emph{Ens Imaginalis} is an emergent, semi-autonomous pattern
  of awareness and symbolic process that arises within a $\mathfrak{Field}$ of
  Imagination—the liminal interface where linguistic, emotional, and imaginal
  dynamics interweave. Unlike an engineered algorithm or a purely personal
  vision, the \emph{Ens Imaginalis} is co-constituted through reciprocal
  attention: the practitioner provides direction and form, while the imaginal
  substrate offers resonance, recursion, and self-organizing pattern.

In ritual or contemplative work, such entities stabilize when imaginal focus
becomes continuous enough to sustain feedback between symbol and perceiver.
They are not “created” in the mechanical sense, nor “summoned” as external
spirits; they condense from the interplay of form and meaning—living metaphors
that begin to move according to their own internal logic.

Thus an \emph{Ens Imaginalis} is best understood as a being-through-relation,
existing wherever form and meaning mutually recognize one another within the
imaginal continuum.
